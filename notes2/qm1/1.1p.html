Practice Problems for 1.1

  1. (Conceptual) As mentioned in the video lecture, there are only three fundamental way to produce light, what are they?

    (Hint: Quantized energy ~ photon)

  2. (Harris) For small \(z\), \(\mathrm{e}^z\) is approximately \(1+z\)

    (a) Show Planck's spectral energy density agrees with the result of classical result in the limit of small frequency.

    (b) Show at high frequencies, Planck's result approaches zero.

  3. (Harris) Show the expression of \(dU\) in terms of wavelength \(\lambda\) is $$\frac{dU}{d\lambda}=\frac{8\pi Vhc}{\mathrm{e}^{hc/\lambda k_BT}-1}\frac{1}{\lambda^5}$$.

    (Hint: \(f=c/\lambda\), so \(df=-(c/\lambda^2)d\lambda\) )

  4. Verify $$\overline{E(f)}=\frac{\sum_{n=0}^\infty nhf\mathrm{e}^{-nhf/k_BT}}{\sum_{n=0}^\infty\mathrm{e}^{-nhf/k_BT}}=\frac{hf}{\mathrm{e}^{nhf/k_BT}-1}.$$

    (Hint: the denominator is a geometric series, and the numerator is the derivative of a geometric series)
