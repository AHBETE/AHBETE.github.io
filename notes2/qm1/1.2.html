1.2 The Photoelectric Effect

Image from Wikipedia

  • Photoelectric effect: Light producing a flow of electricity upon a matel surface.

  • Work function \(\phi\): the minimum energy required to free an electron.

  • Stopping potential: the potential difference that just enough to stops electrons.

  • Threshold wavelength: the maximum wavelength for which electrons are freed. Corresponding minimum frequency is the threshold frequency.

  • New energy unit - electronvolt: an electronvolt (\(eV\)) is the amount of kinetic energy gained by a single electron accelerating through an electric potential difference of one volt in vacuum. Conversion: $$1eV=1.602\times10^{-19}J.$$

  • Energy of photons$$\bbox[silver]{E=hf}.$$

  • Maximum possible kinetic energy of electrons $$\bbox[silver]{K.E._{\text{max}}=hf-\phi}.$$
