Practice Problems for 1.3

  1. (Harris) An X-ray of unknown wavelength is directed at a carbon sample (source of electron). An electron is scattered with a speed of \(4.5\times10^{7}\) m/s at an angle of \(60^\circ\). Determine the wavelength of the X-ray source.

  2. (Harris) Obtain $$\lambda'-\lambda=\frac{h}{m_e c}(1-\cos(\theta)),$$ from $$\begin{cases}\frac{h}{\lambda}=\frac{h}{\lambda'}\cos(\theta)+\gamma_u m_e u\cos(\phi),\\0=\frac{h}{\lambda'}\sin(\theta)-\gamma_u m_e u\sin(\phi),\\h\frac{c}{\lambda}+m_ec^2=h\frac{c}{\lambda'}+\gamma_u m_ec^2.\end{cases}$$

    (Hint: use \(\cos^2(\phi)+\sin^2(\phi)=1\))

  3. (Harris) Show the scattering angles of the photon and the electron following $$\cot(\frac{\theta}{2})=\left(1+\frac{h}{mc\lambda}\right)\tan(\phi).$$
